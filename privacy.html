Cookies and Tracking Technologies

What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device when you visit our Services. They help us recognize you, remember your preferences, and understand how you use our Services. We use both session-based and persistent cookies.

Types of Cookies We Use

  • Essential Cookies: Required for the basic functionality of our Services, such as maintaining your session and processing transactions.
  • Performance Cookies: Help us understand how visitors use our Services by collecting and reporting information anonymously.
  • Functional Cookies: Remember your preferences and enable personalized features based on your previous interactions.
  • Marketing Cookies: Track your activity across websites to deliver targeted advertising and measure campaign effectiveness.

Other Tracking Technologies

In addition to cookies, we use other tracking technologies including:

  • Web Beacons: Tiny images embedded in emails and web pages to track whether content has been viewed.
  • Pixels: Small code snippets that track user interactions and conversions.
  • Local Storage: Browser-based storage that persists data across sessions without user interaction.
  • Analytics Tools: Third-party services that help us understand user behavior and website performance.

Managing Your Cookie Preferences

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can choose to reject or delete cookies, though this may affect the functionality of our Services. You can also opt out of certain types of tracking by adjusting your browser settings or using opt-out tools provided by third-party services. Please note that disabling cookies may limit your ability to use certain features of our Services.
